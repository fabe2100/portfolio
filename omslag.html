Art Magazine Spread

This project showcases a two-page spread designed for an art magazine, focusing on the use of typography to enhance the visual narrative and create a cohesive reading experience about oil painting.

Typographic Choices

The design employs three typefaces, each chosen for a specific purpose:

Helvetica

Used for the main headline. Its modern and simple design provides a strong visual anchor. It captures the reader's attention and introduces the article's topic: "OLJEMÃ…LNINGEN MELLAN: Tradition och Modernitet".

Futura

Used for captions and the pull quote ("Genom oljans magi..."). Its geometric form highlights specific aspects of the content and adds a contemporary feel.

Baskerville

Used for the body text. Its elegance and high readability make it suitable for extended reading, providing a contrast to the modern Helvetica and Futura.

Design Principles

The design adheres to principles of clear communication and visual harmony:

  • Symmetrical Layout: The layout is arranged around a vertical axis, creating an elegant and balanced composition.
  • Typographic Hierarchy: Achieved through variations in font size, weight (regular, bold, italic), and the use of small caps. The sizes follow a modified Fibonacci sequence (34pt, 21pt, 13pt, 8pt) to create visual harmony.
  • Readability: Optimized by adjusting leading (vertical space between lines) to enhance the reading experience and create "active air."
  • Image-Text Integration: Images of oil paintings reinforce the text's theme, adhering to the concept of anchorage, where text and image work together to convey the message.
