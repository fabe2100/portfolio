An analyse of Kivras usability that leads to a designkoncept and an interactive prototype with new added feautures.

Background and Goals

This project involved analyzing Kivra to identify usability weaknesses and strengths, aiming to improve the concept and user experience.

  • Overall Goal: Improve usability and user satisfaction.
  • UX Goals: Simplify navigation and clarify features.
  • Target Audience Needs: Easy-to-use, efficient, and accessible design.

My Role

In this project, I was responsible for:

  • UX Design
  • User Research
  • Information Architecture
  • Prototype Design (low-fidelity and high-fidelity)
  • Usability Testing
  • Evaluation using Benyon's 12 points

Design Process

Analysis

Conducted user analysis and research to understand pain points and opportunities.

Sketches

Created sketches and wireframes to explore design ideas and layouts.

Prototype

Developed interactive prototypes to test and refine the design.

Concept

Finalized the design concept based on feedback and testing.

Results and Learnings

Results

  • Usability tests showed improved user-friendliness, aesthetics, and efficiency (according to Benyon's 12 points).
  • Identified areas for improvement include customization options and clearer explanations of some features.
  • The app is assessed to have the potential to attract users and offer a positive experience.

Learnings

Throughout the project, I learned the importance of early usability testing and iterative design. I also realized how crucial it is to continuously evaluate design decisions based on user needs and established UX principles.

In future projects, I would like to explore further customization options and ensure clearer communication of all features to minimize confusion.
