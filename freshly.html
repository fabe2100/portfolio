Visual Identity for Freshly

  • Logo Design
  • Packaging
  • Typography
  • Brand Identity
  • Print Design
  • Adobe InDesign

This project involved the development of a comprehensive visual identity for "Freshly," a fictional company specializing in fresh-pressed juices. The core objective was to create a brand identity that effectively communicates the company's values of freshness, health, and natural ingredients.

Company Overview

Freshly was established in 1998 by brothers Hans and Adam Nilsson in Stockholm, with the vision of becoming an international company. The company is committed to producing high-quality, fresh-pressed juices, offering a healthy beverage option in the market. Freshly's products emphasize natural ingredients, blending fruits and vegetables to create nutrient-rich drinks. The company prioritizes organic ingredients to minimize environmental impact and provide customers with the most nutritious products.

Logo Design

The logo was designed to serve as the visual cornerstone of Freshly's brand identity. It incorporates a rounded symbol that merges elements of fruit and vegetables, alongside the company name "Freshly". The design is intended to evoke a sense of nature and freshness. Inspiration for the logo was drawn from various sources, including the structural composition of a fruit logo by Inkyy, the use of fruit symbols and text placement in a logo by Khadiza Binte Harun, and the name inspiration from the "Greenly" logo by Onripus. The logo development process involved sketching and refining different variations to achieve the final design.

Typography

The selection of typography was crucial for creating a harmonious and effective visual communication. Maiandra GD was chosen for the logo to complement the symbol and other graphic elements. Sans-serif typefaces were prioritized for their readability and modern aesthetic. Montserrat is used for headings, while Roboto is used for body text, ensuring clarity and hierarchy across different applications.

Color Palette

The color palette is inspired by nature, reflecting the fruits and vegetables used in Freshly's products. The colors were carefully chosen to reinforce the brand's association with fresh ingredients and create a visually appealing experience. The palette includes primary colors (HEX 53924D, HEX EA4335) and secondary colors (HEX 4CAF50, HEX DBF9B8, HEX FFF533, HEX FC6B2A, HEX 45260E).

Graphic Element

A line of leaves was created as a distinctive graphic element to reinforce the brand's message of health and naturalness. The leaves symbolize freshness and are used consistently across various materials, including business cards and packaging, to create a cohesive visual identity.

Applications

Business Card

The business card design incorporates the logo, contact information, and the leaf graphic element to provide a unified brand experience. Inspiration for the business card design was drawn from Mike Christou's design, particularly the layout of contact information and the use of a green color scheme.

Packaging

The juice packaging design features the logo, product name, description, ingredients, and nutritional information, effectively communicating the company's vision and product details. The packaging design, inspired by the use of green tones in Rita's juice packaging, aims to be informative and visually appealing.

Presentation

The presentation, created in Affinity Publisher, maintains a clean and organized layout with ample white space to emphasize the graphic elements. The layout is designed to be consistent across all materials to enhance brand recognition.

Design Process and Reflection

The design process emphasized creating a visual identity that aligns with Freshly's core values and resonates with the target audience seeking a healthy lifestyle. Research and inspiration from other designs played a crucial role in shaping the visual elements. Key considerations included color psychology, typography, and composition to ensure effective communication and brand recognition. Challenges were encountered in focusing on the target audience, but inspiration helped in creating a matching vision for the company. A key learning was the importance of consistency across all design elements to establish a strong and cohesive brand identity.
